# Spectral Freeze and Delay
<img width="742" height="349" alt="Screenshot 2026-03-10 123233" src="https://github.com/user-attachments/assets/dc409c7c-bd4d-4ad4-9b8a-f25b4d9610cb" />

SpectralFreeze is an audio effect that captures and sustains the spectral content of an incoming signal in real time. Instead of freezing the waveform itself, the plugin analyzes the signal in the frequency domain and holds its spectral structure, allowing the captured sound to be prolonged, transformed, and blended with the original input.

In addition to freezing the spectrum, SpectralFreeze also provides a spectral grid delay system. The analyzed spectrum is divided into multiple frequency bands, each of which can be assigned its own delay time. This allows different parts of the spectrum to evolve independently over time, creating complex rhythmic and timbral structures. Low, mid, and high frequency components of a sound can therefore be delayed by different amounts, producing shifting spectral echoes and evolving textures.
